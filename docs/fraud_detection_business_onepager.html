The Problem Zilch Is Trying to Solve

Fraudsters test stolen cards in rapid bursts — often 5–10 low-value transactions within seconds. If your fraud model only sees stale features, every transaction in a burst looks clean until it's too late. The loss isn't just the fraudulent spend: it's chargebacks, issuer fees, reputational risk with Thredd, and the engineering cost of building the detection capability from scratch.

Business Impact
🛡
Faster Detection = Less Fraud Loss
Card-testing bursts are visible from the second transaction — within 2 seconds of the first event. A model on stale features cannot see the burst pattern. Every transaction that slips through is a confirmed fraud loss plus chargeback.
⚙
Engineering Cost Reduction
Replacing 10 independently operated services with one platform eliminates cross-service incident response and version management overhead. Fewer services = fewer pages, less on-call burden. That headcount redeploys to fraud model iteration.
⚡
Faster Model Iteration
With training data, features, and serving on one platform, the cycle from new fraud signal identified to model in production compresses from weeks to days. Faster iteration directly improves catch rates as attack patterns evolve.
